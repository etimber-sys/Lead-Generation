# LMU Family Business Program — Implementation Strategy
**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Audience:** Director, LMU Family Business Entrepreneurship Program
**Objective:** Improve both program growth (membership) and program quality (member experience) with a primary constraint of low awareness in the LA market.

---

## Strategic Architecture

The strategy runs on three parallel tracks that compound over time, with program quality improvements running underneath all three.

**Track A — Outbound:** Activate the 1,445-business compiled CSV through persona-segmented LinkedIn + email outreach sequences. Goal is not immediate membership conversion — it is to drive prospects to a free, low-barrier touchpoint (event or lead magnet). Every sequence ends with a single CTA.

**Track B — Referral & Partner Network:** Build a referral engine using three channels — current members, LMU CBA alumni, and professional advisors (CPAs, estate attorneys, wealth managers) who already serve family businesses. Each channel gets a specific ask and a clear incentive.

**Track C — Content & SEO:** Build durable inbound traffic by optimizing LMU's existing web presence for LA-specific family business keywords, launching a newsletter (every two weeks), and publishing pillar content targeting the top pain points from persona research.

**Core insight from research:** Family businesses require 3–5 touchpoints before committing to a program. Every track is designed to feed prospects into a trust-building nurture sequence before asking for membership.

## Section 1: Quick Wins (Months 0–3)

### Month 1 — Outbound Launch
- Segment the 1,445-business CSV into three persona buckets:
  - **Founders** (55–70, CEO/owner titles)
  - **Successors** (28–45, next-gen titles)
  - **Sibling Teams** (co-founders/co-owners with shared surnames)
- Build a 3-step LinkedIn outreach sequence per persona:
  1. Connection request (no pitch)
  2. Value message referencing a specific pain point from persona research
  3. CTA to a free event or downloadable resource
- Use persona-specific pain point language from the research (e.g., succession anxiety for Founders; authority/identity for Successors; role clarity for Sibling Teams)

### Month 1 — Lead Magnet: Succession Readiness Assessment
- Build a 10-question self-scoring tool: "How Ready Is Your Family Business for the Next Generation?"
- Gate with name + email only (more than 3 fields reduces conversion 40–60%)
- Every download enters a 4-email automated nurture sequence ending with an invitation to the next forum event
- Format: interactive tool (converts at 20–30% vs. ~15% for static PDFs)

### Month 2 — Referral Program Activation
- Contact current members: refer one family business → $150 membership credit
- Provide a one-paragraph referral script and a direct tracking link
- Identify 10–15 LA-area CPAs, estate attorneys, and wealth managers
- Offer co-branded content (checklist or webinar) in exchange for introductions — positions the program as complementary to their advisory services, not competitive

### Month 3 — Google Business Profile + 3 SEO Pages
- Optimize Google Business Profile: photos, event listings, keyword-rich description
- Publish three new web pages:
  1. "Family Business Program Los Angeles"
  2. "Family Business Succession Planning Help"
  3. "Next Generation Leadership Development"
- Include schema markup (Business type, events, FAQ) and local testimonials

## Section 2: Program Quality Improvements

### Structured Peer Groups (Highest Priority)
- Launch three tiered peer groups: Founders, Next-Gen Leaders, Sibling Teams
- Quarterly meetings (virtual or in-person), 90 minutes, facilitated
- Capped at 8–12 members per group; confidentiality is a stated norm
- Rationale: peer connection is the #1 stated need across all three personas; every top competitor program (UNI, UNH, St. Thomas) uses this as a core membership benefit

### Curriculum Mapped to Top 10 Pain Points
Publish a content calendar that explicitly links each event/workshop to a ranked member pain point:
1. No succession plan
2. Unclear successor selection
3. Generational conflict
4. Lack of family communication
5. Founder control issues
6. Compensation inequity
7. Clan culture / merit gaps
8. Tax & estate complexity
9. No governance structure
10. Leadership vacuum risk

This makes the value of membership concrete and reduces the "I don't know if this applies to us" objection.

### Member Feedback Loop
- 3-question pulse survey after every event:
  1. What was most valuable?
  2. What was missing?
  3. Would you refer someone based on today?
- Review quarterly; use verbatim responses as testimonial language in marketing

### Student Integration
- Create a structured role for LMU entrepreneurship students supporting member companies: research projects, event assistance, internship pipelines
- Position as a named membership benefit: "Priority access to LMU students"
- Benefits both members (free talent) and students (real family business exposure)

### Annual Conference as a Conversion Funnel
- Offer discounted or free tickets to non-members
- Design the conference agenda to surface the program's peer group and curriculum offerings
- Use the Next Gen Leadership Forum model (Feb 2026) as a template: accessible, topic-specific, low-barrier

## Section 3: 12-Month Roadmap

| Phase | Months | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Launch | 1–3 | Activate list, build lead magnet, launch referral program, quick SEO wins |
| Build | 3–6 | Launch peer groups, map curriculum to pain points, establish partner MOUs, grow newsletter |
| Scale | 6–12 | Compound inbound traffic, deepen referral network, publish member case studies, optimize based on data |

### Key Milestones
- **Month 1:** Outreach sequences live for all 3 personas; lead magnet published
- **Month 2:** First referral credits issued; 10 partner relationships initiated
- **Month 3:** Google Business Profile optimized; 3 SEO pages live; first nurture sequence complete
- **Month 4:** Peer groups launched with founding cohorts
- **Month 6:** Newsletter (every two weeks) at 200+ subscribers; curriculum calendar published for H2
- **Month 9:** First member case studies published; referral program generating 2+ leads/month
- **Month 12:** Full stop/start/continue review across all three tracks

## Section 4: Success Metrics

| Goal | Metric | 12-Month Target |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Outreach contacts reached | 500+ from CSV |
| Lead generation | Email subscribers captured | 300+ |
| Conversion | Net new memberships | 20–30 |
| Retention | Member renewal rate | 75%+ |
| Program quality | Post-event satisfaction score | 4.2+/5.0 |
| Referrals | Member-referred leads per quarter | 5+ by Q4 |
| SEO | Organic sessions to program pages | 2x baseline |

### Review Cadence
Evaluate at 3, 6, and 12 months using a "stop, start, or continue" framework for every active tactic. This prevents the director from running tactics that stopped working and keeps the strategy adaptive to what the data shows.
