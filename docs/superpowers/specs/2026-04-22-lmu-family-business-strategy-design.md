# LMU Family Business Program — Implementation Strategy
**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Audience:** Director, LMU Family Business Entrepreneurship Program
**Objective:** Improve both program growth (membership) and program quality (member experience) with a primary constraint of low awareness in the LA market.

---

## Strategic Architecture

The strategy runs on three tracks — but they are not equal in weight or duration.

**Track A — List Activation (Month 1 only):** The compiled CSV contains 41 confirmed family business leads, 22 of whom are LMU alumni. This is a one-time activation play, not a sustained outbound engine. All 41 are contacted personally in Month 1 — alumni first. At a realistic 10–15% conversion rate to membership, this list alone yields 4–6 new members. It is a strong start, not the full answer.

**Track B — Referral & Partner Network (primary sustained growth engine):** Because the existing list is small, the referral and partner network carries the most weight for reaching 8–15 net new memberships. Three channels: current members (incentivized referrals), LMU CBA alumni, and professional advisors — CPAs, estate attorneys, and wealth managers — who already serve family businesses and can introduce the program to clients. This track compounds over time: each new member becomes a potential referral source.

**Track C — Content & SEO (durable inbound engine):** Builds a pipeline that doesn't depend on who the director knows or who's already in the CSV. Optimizing LMU's web presence for LA-specific family business keywords, launching a biweekly newsletter, and publishing pillar content creates a stream of inbound leads that grows independently. Results take 60–90 days to appear but are self-sustaining once built.

**Program quality runs underneath all three tracks.** Retention, referrals, and word-of-mouth all depend on whether members find the program valuable. A member who renews is worth more than a new one acquired. A member who refers is a multiplier.

**How 8–15 memberships get reached:**
- 4–6 from the existing 41-lead CSV (Track A, Month 1)
- 2–4 from referrals by current members and advisor introductions (Track B, Months 2–9)
- 2–5 from inbound via lead magnet, SEO, and conference attendance (Track C, Months 3–12)

**Core insight from research:** Family businesses require 3–5 touchpoints before committing to a program. Every track feeds prospects into a trust-building nurture sequence before asking for membership.

## Section 1: Quick Wins (Months 0–3)

### Month 1 — Activate the Existing List (Track A)

**Wave 1 (Weeks 1–2): LMU Alumni (22 leads)**
- Reach out personally via LinkedIn or direct email — reference the LMU connection explicitly
- CTA: invitation to the next forum event or the Succession Readiness Assessment, not a membership pitch
- Personal outreach from the program director carries significant weight with alumni

**Wave 2 (Weeks 3–4): Non-alumni confirmed leads (19 leads)**
- Personalized outreach referencing their specific business or industry
- Same CTA approach: event or lead magnet, not membership

**This list is exhausted by end of Month 1. List-building begins simultaneously and continues indefinitely:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator: filter LA-area businesses with family-owned indicators, 10–500 employees, owner/founder titles (~10–15 new leads per week reviewed)
- Advisory board and current member nominations: "Who else should be in this room?"
- Inbound leads captured via lead magnet and SEO pages (Track C) as they come online

### Month 1 — Lead Magnet: Succession Readiness Assessment

- Build a 10-question self-scoring tool: "How Ready Is Your Family Business for the Next Generation?"
- Gate with name + email only (more than 3 fields reduces conversion 40–60%)
- Every completion triggers a 4-email automated nurture sequence ending with a membership CTA
- Format: interactive tool (converts at 20–30% vs. ~15% for static PDFs)
- This becomes the primary top-of-funnel asset for both Track B partner referrals and Track C inbound traffic

### Month 2 — Referral Program Activation (Track B)

- Contact current members: refer one family business → $150 membership credit
- Provide a one-paragraph referral script and a tracking link — remove all friction
- Identify 10–15 LA-area CPAs, estate attorneys, and wealth managers
- Offer a co-branded checklist or co-hosted event in exchange for client introductions — positions the program as complementary to their advisory services, not competitive
- **This is the primary growth lever given the small starting list size**

### Month 3 — Google Business Profile + 3 SEO Pages (Track C)

- Optimize Google Business Profile: photos, event listings, keyword-rich description
- Publish three new web pages targeting high-intent LA searches:
  1. "Family Business Program Los Angeles"
  2. "Family Business Succession Planning Help"
  3. "Next Generation Leadership Development"
- Include schema markup (Business type, events, FAQ) and local member testimonials
- These pages feed inbound leads into the lead magnet and nurture sequence permanently

## Section 2: Program Quality Improvements

### Structured Peer Groups (Highest Priority)

- Launch three tiered peer groups: Founders, Next-Gen Leaders, Sibling Teams
- Quarterly meetings (virtual or in-person), 90 minutes, facilitated
- Capped at 8–12 members per group; confidentiality is a stated norm
- Minimum viable group: 5 members — don't wait for 8–12 to launch
- Rationale: peer connection is the #1 stated need across all three personas; every top competitor program (UNI, UNH, St. Thomas) uses this as a core membership benefit; it is also the most powerful retention driver and word-of-mouth generator

### Curriculum Mapped to Top 10 Pain Points

Publish a content calendar that explicitly links each event/workshop to a ranked member pain point:
1. No succession plan
2. Unclear successor selection
3. Generational conflict
4. Lack of family communication
5. Founder control issues
6. Compensation inequity
7. Clan culture / merit gaps
8. Tax & estate complexity
9. No governance structure
10. Leadership vacuum risk

This makes the value of membership concrete and reduces the "I don't know if this applies to us" objection before it's raised.

### Member Feedback Loop

- 3-question pulse survey after every event:
  1. What was most valuable?
  2. What was missing?
  3. Would you refer someone based on today?
- Review quarterly; use verbatim responses as testimonial language in marketing
- High referral-intent responses (Q3 ≥ 9/10) are priority candidates for case studies

### Student Integration

- Create a structured role for LMU entrepreneurship students supporting member companies: research projects, event assistance, internship pipelines
- Position as a named membership benefit: "Priority access to LMU students"
- Benefits both members (free talent, fresh perspective) and students (real family business exposure)

### Annual Conference as Conversion Funnel

- Offer discounted or free tickets to non-members — the conference is a marketing event as much as a member benefit
- Design the agenda to surface peer group and curriculum offerings explicitly
- Use the Next Gen Leadership Forum model (Feb 2026) as a template: accessible, topic-specific, low-barrier
- Track how many non-member attendees convert to membership within 90 days of attending

## Section 3: 12-Month Roadmap

| Phase | Months | Primary Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Launch | 1–3 | Exhaust existing list; launch lead magnet + nurture; activate referral program; quick SEO wins |
| Build | 3–6 | Launch peer groups; map curriculum to pain points; establish partner MOUs; grow newsletter to 100+ subscribers |
| Scale | 6–12 | Compound inbound traffic; deepen referral network; publish member case studies; optimize based on quarterly reviews |

### Key Milestones
- **Month 1:** All 41 CSV leads personally contacted (alumni wave first); lead magnet live; list-building underway
- **Month 2:** Referral program launched to all current members; 10 advisor outreach contacts made
- **Month 3:** Google Business Profile optimized; 3 SEO pages submitted to web team; first nurture sequence complete
- **Month 4:** All three peer groups launched with founding cohorts (5+ members each)
- **Month 6:** Newsletter at 100+ subscribers; curriculum calendar published for H2; 2+ advisor partnerships active
- **Month 9:** First 2 member case studies published; referral program generating 2+ leads/month
- **Month 12:** Full stop/start/continue review; assess whether 8–15 membership target is on track

Evaluate at 3, 6, and 12 months using a "stop, start, or continue" framework for every active tactic. Given the small starting list, early signals from Track B (referral responses, advisor engagement) and Track C (newsletter open rates, assessment completions) are the leading indicators to watch most closely.
