**Source:** Search results on how university family business programs recruit members
**Research Date:** April 2026

## University of Northern Iowa (UNI) — Family Business Center
**URL:** https://unifamilybusinesscenter.com/
**Location:** Cedar Falls, Iowa

### How They Recruit Family Businesses

#### Primary Entry Points
- **Main CTA:** "Join the Community" — direct link to membership application
- **Referral Program:** $150 discount for referrer + thank-you gift for bringing in a new member
- **Free Trial Hook:** Referral leads get access to one free Virtual Breakfast Series session + monthly newsletter before committing to membership
- **Contact:** Jayne Kielman (jayne.kielman@uni.edu, 319-273-4333)

#### Outreach Channels
- Monthly Iowa Family Business Newsletter (used as both lead magnet and nurture tool)
- Virtual Breakfast Series (free first session as low-barrier entry point)
- Peer Groups (three tiers: Current Leadership, Next-Gen, Family Office Directors)
- Fall Family Business Conference (free/discounted tickets for members)
- YouTube, Instagram, Spotify (podcast), LinkedIn, Facebook

#### Messaging Strategy
- **Tagline:** "Your Family. Your Legacy. Our Community."
- **Secondary tagline:** "Join a network of local families driving global impact."
- Emphasis on keeping business local while scaling influence
- "Iowa Family Stories" section with real member case studies (Nutriom, King's Materials, Kelly family/CCR, Klauer Manufacturing)
- **Member testimonial (Mike Klauer):** "I have thoroughly enjoyed getting to know the UNI Family Business Center and their member companies. Iowa is home to so many wonderful family businesses... The UNI Family Business Center does a wonderful job of bringing these businesses together with valuable content and opportunity for collaboration."

#### Member Value Props Used in Recruitment
- 5 Virtual Breakfast Series sessions
- Fall Family Business Conference tickets
- Tour & Talk sessions
- Generational Peer Groups (3 virtual groups)
- Unlimited access to webinars & resource bank
- Virtual and in-person consulting discounts
- Board matchmaking services
- Biography & memoir writing services (national members only)

## Cross-Program Lead Generation Patterns

### The Tiered CTA Hierarchy Used by Top Programs
1. **Free/Low-barrier entry:** Free webinar, free first session, monthly newsletter signup
2. **Interest form:** Simple contact + demographic info (not a full application)
3. **Peer group inquiry:** Direct email to coordinator
4. **Full membership application:** Formal application form
5. **Referral incentive:** Clear benefit for existing members who refer

### Recruitment vs. Outreach Distinction (from ETA/DOL Framework)
- **Recruitment** = placing ads, media, social media (can be done remotely)
- **Outreach** = face-to-face relationship building, community partnerships (requires dedicated person)
- Measurement cadence: evaluate at 3 months, 6 months, 12 months — ask "stop, start, or continue"

### Key Tactics Used Across Programs
- Community-based organization (CBO) partnerships
- Leveraging board members and advisory committees as referral sources
- Elevator pitch ready for all staff and board
- Focus groups with past/current members to understand their decision journey
- MOUs (Memorandums of Understanding) with partner organizations
- Success stories and member testimonials as social proof
- Alumni networks as referral sources
- "Hire Locally" and community impact messaging

### Social Media Recruitment Language Examples
- "Be more than just an employee. Make a difference in someone's life."
- Focus on authentic, user-generated content from existing members
- Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn most effective; TikTok emerging

### Passive vs. Proactive Outreach
- **Passive:** flyers, social media posts, tabling at events
- **Proactive:** 1:1 outreach, direct email/LinkedIn messaging, class/event presentations
- **Quality over quantity:** build relationships not just sign-ups
- Learn goals, address barriers (time, cost, relevance) in direct conversations

## Recommended CTAs for LMU Family Business Program

Based on competitor analysis, the most effective CTA hierarchy for a university family business program:

| Priority | CTA | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Entry | Free newsletter signup / free event access | Email capture only |
| Low friction | "Download our Family Business Guide" | Email + 2 questions |
| Medium | "Join an Info Session" | Name + email + company |
| High intent | "Apply for Membership" | Full qualification form |
| Referral | "Refer a Family Business" | Incentivized referral link |
