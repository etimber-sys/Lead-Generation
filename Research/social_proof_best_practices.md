## What Makes Testimonials Credible & High-Converting

### Must-Have Elements
- **Full name** (first and last)
- **Job title and company name**
- **Headshot photo** (significantly boosts trust)
- **Specific metrics or outcomes** (e.g., "grew revenue 30%", "resolved a 10-year family dispute")
- **Direct quotes** (not paraphrased)

### Timing
- Ask immediately after a positive interaction or event
- Within 24–48 hours of a success milestone
- After annual conference, peer group sessions, or consulting wins

### Format
> "Companies want to witness a change that feels achievable for them, and not just to hear that an agency did a fantastic job. The stories that resonate most are the ones with concrete data, clear before and after results, and industry relevance, because proof of impact always outperforms generic praise." — Mariana Delgado, Head of Marketing, DesignRush

## Case Study Structure for B2B (Problem-Solution-Results)

**1. Problem** — What challenge did the family business face?
- Be specific: succession conflict, leadership gap, stalled growth

**2. Solution** — What did the LMU program provide?
- Peer group insight, workshop, annual conference connection, mentorship

**3. Results** — What measurably changed?
- Include specific numbers: revenue, relationships repaired, transition completed, new leader developed

## Video Testimonials vs Written

| Factor | Video | Written |
|---|---|---|
| Trust level | Highest (shows authenticity, body language, emotion) | Medium |
| Production effort | Higher | Lower |
| Shareability | High (LinkedIn, email, website) | High (embed anywhere) |
| Skepticism reduction | Very effective | Moderate |
| Best for | Homepage, key landing pages | Everywhere |

> "Video testimonials add tone, body language, authenticity... Captures emotion in a way written text cannot."

---

## What to Avoid

- Generic testimonials: "Great program!" (no specifics)
- Missing attribution: no name, title, or company
- Outdated case studies (older than 2 years for fast-changing industries)
- Fake reviews or inflated numbers
- Irrelevant awards from unrecognized organizations
- Vanity metrics (social follower counts, generic volume stats)
- Using only 5-star ratings (4.0–4.7 is more credible)

## Recommendations for LMU Family Business Program

### Quick Wins
1. Add member testimonials with names, titles, company names, and photos to the membership page
2. Place a member count stat prominently: "100+ family enterprises" or "X members since 2021"
3. Feature one detailed case study per key target segment (e.g., manufacturing, retail, professional services)
4. Add logos of recognizable member companies (San Antonio Winery, Gaviña Coffee, Kirk Kara)

### Medium-Term
5. Record 2–3 short video testimonials from members after the annual conference
6. Create a "Member Stories" or "Family Business Spotlight" section with narrative case studies
7. Collect G2/third-party reviews if applicable, or add to Google Business Profile

### Placement Priority
- Homepage: member count stat + logo strip + 2–3 short text testimonials
- Membership page: detailed case study + ROI-focused testimonials + video (if available)
- Events page: post-event testimonials and recap quotes
- Near membership CTA: 1 short, specific testimonial from a recognizable business
